<p class="lede">Client-site packing should be boring. The goal is to open the laptop, connect to the room, take notes, and avoid performing a small cable tragedy before the meeting starts.</p>

{% include stat-band.html stats=page.testing.stats %}

<h2><span class="sec">01</span>The pouch is the system</h2>
<p>The six-item kit is charger, cable, hub, earbuds, pen, and pouch. The restraint matters. The moment a second adapter, backup cable, and spare pen join the party, the pouch stops being a system and becomes a drawer with a zipper.</p>

<h2><span class="sec">02</span>Who should skip it</h2>
<p>If your work never leaves a fixed desk, skip the kit and build a better desk drawer. This is for client rooms, conference rooms, and borrowed desks where competence is partly measured by how little setup drama you create.</p>
