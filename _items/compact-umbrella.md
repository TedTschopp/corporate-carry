## Verdict

The Compact Umbrella is the lightweight travel pick for people who need rain
coverage without dedicating a full side pocket to weather. It is less robust
than the Metro umbrella, but easier to justify in a client-week bag.

## Best for

Use it for airport days, hotel-to-client walks, and trips where the forecast is
uncertain enough to punish optimism. At 175 g, it can stay packed without
making the travel kit feel overbuilt.

## What it solves

The failure is arriving at a client site damp, distracted, and visibly less
prepared than you meant to be. Weather is not always controllable. Looking like
you ignored it usually is.

## What worked

The compact format matters because business travel already has enough objects.
This umbrella gives useful coverage while staying small enough to live in the
bag until needed.

## What did not

It is not as robust as the Stratus Forge Metro pick. If your commute regularly
includes wind and heavy weather, choose the stronger umbrella and accept the
cost.

## Final call

Carry it for travel backup. Skip it if weather is a daily commute problem and
you need a stronger tool.
