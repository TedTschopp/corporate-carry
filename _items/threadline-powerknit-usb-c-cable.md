## Verdict

The Threadline PowerKnit USB-C Cable is the cable we would rather stop
thinking about. It carries 100W, uses a braided jacket, and stays short enough
for a pouch without becoming a hotel-room tripwire.

## Best for

Use it as the primary cable paired with the Voltforge 735 Nano II Charger. It
belongs in a tech pouch, client-site kit, or travel power setup where the rule
is simple: charger and cable stay together.

## What it solves

Most cable problems are not dramatic. They are the small daily failures of the
wrong length, wrong output, or wrong bag. A reliable 100W cable removes one
branch from that decision tree.

## What worked

The 1 m length is right for desk and conference-room use when the charger is
nearby. The braided jacket survived pouch rotation without kinking into a
shape that argues with the rest of the kit.

## What did not

One meter is not enough for every hotel outlet. If your travel week often
requires reaching behind beds or across hostile desks, pack a longer cable in
the travel kit and keep this one paired with the daily charger.

## Final call

Carry one dependable cable and make it live with the charger. The productivity
gain is not speed. It is never having to ask which cable you packed.
