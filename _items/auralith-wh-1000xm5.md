## Verdict

The Auralith WH-1000XM5 is the pick when quiet is part of the job. It is a
full-size headphone for open offices, flights, and train commutes where earbuds
are not enough to turn noise into usable work time.

## Best for

Use it for flight weeks, loud office zones, long focus blocks, and calls that
move between laptop and phone. Multipoint pairing is not glamorous, but it
removes the little Bluetooth dance that makes people late to calls.

## What it solves

The problem is not music. The problem is control. A noisy gate, busy train, or
open office can turn a 30-minute work block into pretend productivity. Strong
active noise cancellation gives that time back.

## What worked

The 30 h battery life is enough for a travel week if you charge with some
discipline. The 250 g weight is noticeable but reasonable for over-ear audio,
and the sound isolation is good enough that the bag space feels justified on
flight days.

## What did not

Full-size headphones take real space. They are the wrong default for a light
office sling and too much if your office is calm. For quick calls and short
commutes, Orbital Cloudbuds are the easier carry.

## Final call

Pack them when quiet has a job to do. Skip them when earbuds already solve the
day. The tradeoff is volume in the bag; the payoff is usable time in bad
rooms.
