## Verdict

Gridbook is the digital planning profile for work that has to leave the
notebook and become visible to other people. It defines one side of the
paper-system decision: when capture needs to become shared work.

## Best for

Use it for project planning, shared notes, and client-week structure that has
to leave the notebook. It is most useful when paired with the Inkplane Tablet,
where capture and review can move between handwriting and digital planning.

## What it solves

Paper is excellent for capture. It is weaker when the note has to become a
task list, project map, or handoff. Gridbook exists for that transition.

## What worked

The useful role is visibility. A planning space that other people can see
keeps project structure from staying trapped in one person's notebook.

## What did not

It is unnecessary for private notes and too heavy a process for small meeting
details. If your paper notes already have a reliable review habit, adding a
workspace can create another place to check.

## Final call

Use it when handwritten capture needs a digital destination. Skip it if your
paper notes already have a reliable review habit.
