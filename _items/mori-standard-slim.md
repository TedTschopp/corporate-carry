## Verdict

The Mori Standard Slim is a meeting pouch, not a tech pouch. That distinction
is the whole recommendation. It gives a pen, pocket notebook, sticky notes,
and small recovery items one place to live without pretending to organize a
charger kit.

## Best for

Use it for client visits, office days, and conference rooms where a small
stationery kit should be reachable from the top of the bag. Pair it with the
Rellan pen or Ledgerline notebook.

## What it solves

The failure is loose meeting gear: pen at the bottom, notebook in another
pocket, sticky notes crushed beside a cable. The slim pouch creates one job and
keeps the items together.

## What worked

At 40 g, it barely changes the bag weight. The slim format is also the point:
it discourages the pouch from becoming a miscellaneous drawer.

## What did not

It is not structured enough for a full tech kit. Chargers, hubs, and cables
belong in the Northline Atelier Classic Tech Pouch.

## Final call

Use it when the pouch has one job. Skip it if you are looking for general
organization. General is how bags get messy again.
