## Verdict

The Stratus Forge Metro Umbrella is the stronger weather-insurance pick for
commutes and client visits where rain is a recurring problem, not a rare
travel surprise. It is expensive for an umbrella, but it earns the spot when
weather failure would be visible.

## Best for

Use it for train commutes, city walks, client visits, and travel days where a
cheap umbrella usually becomes a one-use object. It belongs in a side pocket
or travel bag where it can stay packed without being forgotten at the first
sign of sun.

## What it solves

The failure is arriving wet, late, and annoyed because the weather plan was
hope. A reinforced compact umbrella keeps that from becoming part of the
meeting.

## What worked

The reinforced canopy is the reason to choose it over a lighter backup. It is
still compact at 175 g, but more confidence-inspiring when wind enters the
conversation.

## What did not

The price is the tradeoff. If you lend umbrellas freely or rarely face bad
weather, the cheaper Compact Umbrella is enough.

## Final call

Buy it if weather is a pattern. Skip it if rain is only an occasional travel
edge case.
