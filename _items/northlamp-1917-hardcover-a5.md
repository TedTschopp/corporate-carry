## Verdict

The Northlamp 1917 Hardcover A5 is the meeting notebook for people who need
paper to survive more than one conversation. Numbered pages, an index, dot
grid format, and a binding that lies flat make it useful beyond the first week
of enthusiasm.

## Best for

Use it for meeting notes, project tracking, planning sessions, and client work
where a pocket notebook is too small. It belongs in a backpack, desk bag, or
meeting pouch with a real pen.

## What it solves

The failure mode is scattered capture: notes in 3 places, decisions lost in a
chat thread, and project context that disappears between meetings. The A5
format gives enough room to write like an adult without turning the notebook
into a desk-only object.

## What worked

The index and numbered pages make review realistic. Dot grid paper handles
lists, diagrams, and rough planning without forcing a layout. The hardcover
adds enough structure for writing away from a desk.

## What did not

At 250 g, it is heavier than pocket notebooks. If your need is quick capture
only, Ledgerline is easier to keep in a slim meeting pouch.

## Final call

Choose it when paper is part of the operating model, not a prop. Skip it if
your notes rarely need to survive the meeting.
