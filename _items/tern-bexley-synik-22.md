## Verdict

The Tern Bexley Synik 22 is the travel operating model bag. It is too large
for a normal hybrid week and exactly right when the calendar includes flights,
client rooms, hotel desks, and enough uncertainty to punish a smaller carry.

## Best for

Use it for a 4-day client week, a weekly flight rhythm, or heavier work setups
that include a larger laptop, over-ear headphones, a power bank, and a paper
system. It is built for movement between states, not just movement between
conference rooms.

## What it solves

Travel bags fail when every item becomes a negotiation. The Synik 22 gives the
power bank, headphones, laptop, and small kit enough separation that airport
and hotel transitions stop becoming public repacking exercises.

## What worked

The 22 L capacity is useful because it is organized capacity. The bag gives
the laptop a protected lane, keeps flight gear reachable, and still has room
for the objects that make a hotel desk workable. It also fits the practical
truth of travel: the item you need next is rarely the one you packed last.

## What did not

It is too much bag for a two-day office week. Carrying it half-empty makes the
whole setup feel oversized and invites extra objects to move in.

## Final call

Choose it for travel discipline, not everyday minimalism. If your week has one
flight and several client rooms, it earns the weight. If your week is a train
and a hot desk, size down.
